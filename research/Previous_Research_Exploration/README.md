# 🌱 Research Origins

## Where did the journey begin?

Every research journey starts with curiosity.

Before exploring biomimicry, regenerative systems, ecological engineering, and nature-inspired innovation, my interest in sustainability began through hands-on STEM projects and scientific explorations developed during my school years in Mexico.

These early experiences introduced me to concepts related to sustainability, chemistry, materials, environmental responsibility, and product design. Through experimentation, observation, and project-based learning, I began asking questions about how products are made, how resources are used, and how innovation can contribute to a more sustainable future.

While many of these projects were small in scale, they played an important role in shaping my perspective and encouraging me to explore the connections between science, engineering, sustainability, and the natural world.

This section documents some of the projects, ideas, and investigations that helped shape my academic interests and eventually inspired my decision to study Environmental and Energy Systems Engineering.

Looking back, these projects represent more than individual assignments—they represent the first steps of a research journey that continues to evolve today through biomimicry, regenerative systems, and the ongoing development of Planta y Diviértete.

## 🌱 From Learning From Plants to Planta y Diviértete

One of the most important projects in this section is **Learning From Plants: Biomimicry and Future Energy Systems (2026)**.

This project marked the beginning of a broader research journey. While initially focused on understanding how plants could inspire future energy systems and sustainable technologies, it gradually evolved into a larger exploration of biomimicry, regenerative systems, ecological engineering, and human-nature relationships.

Many of the questions explored in this research became the foundation for the ongoing development of **Planta y Diviértete**, a long-term research initiative focused on learning from plants and living ecosystems to design regenerative systems for future communities.

In this sense, *Learning From Plants* represents the origin of the ideas that continue to shape my current research direction.
