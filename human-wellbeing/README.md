# 📁 Human Wellbeing

## Reimagining the Relationship Between Humans and Nature

What if some of the most advanced solutions for the future were not hidden inside laboratories, factories, or computer systems, but growing quietly around us?

For millions of years, plants have been solving challenges that humanity continues to face today. They capture energy directly from the sun, adapt to changing environments, cooperate with surrounding ecosystems, store carbon, regulate climate, and create the conditions necessary for life to thrive. Yet despite their extraordinary abilities, they are often overlooked.

Planta y Diviértete was born from a simple observation: nature is not only something we should protect—it is something we can learn from.

This project is driven by the belief that the future of innovation does not depend solely on creating more technology, but on developing a deeper understanding of the living systems that already sustain our world. Instead of asking how we can take more from nature, this project asks a different question:

**How can we learn from nature and give back more than we take?**

At its heart, Planta y Diviértete is a project about people. It is about helping communities discover opportunities within the resources that already exist around them. It is about demonstrating that meaningful innovation does not always require expensive technologies, but can emerge from understanding the principles that ecosystems have refined over millions of years.

Growing plants for more than six years across Mexico, Canada, and Switzerland has shown me that plants are far more than biological organisms. They are teachers of resilience, adaptation, patience, cooperation, and regeneration. They continuously respond to environmental change while contributing positively to the systems around them.

These observations inspire a broader vision: a future where engineering, design, and innovation work alongside nature rather than apart from it.

I am particularly motivated by the possibility of supporting communities with limited resources, especially in Mexico, by exploring solutions that are accessible, regenerative, and rooted in local environments. I believe that many communities already possess valuable resources, knowledge, and ecological relationships that can become the foundation for positive change.

The goal of this project is not simply to develop new ideas inspired by plants. The goal is to help create a future where human systems become active contributors to ecological regeneration, where innovation strengthens both communities and ecosystems, and where nature is recognized not only as a resource, but as one of humanity's greatest sources of knowledge.

Ultimately, Planta y Diviértete is an invitation to rethink what progress means.

A future built only on extraction will eventually reach its limits.

A future built on regeneration can continue to grow.

By learning from plants, we may discover not only better systems for the planet, but also a better relationship with the living world that makes our existence possible.
